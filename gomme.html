Gomme Syrup

Gomme syrup (also gum syrup) is a rich simple syrup emulsified with gum arabic, giving cocktails a velvety mouthfeel and better texture. It's a staple of classic bartending — used in Old Fashioneds, Whiskey Sours, and daiquiris by those who know.

Method

  1. 01
    Dissolve gum arabic in cold water. Combine the gum arabic powder with a small amount of cold water and let it hydrate for at least 30 minutes (or overnight). It will look clumpy at first — that's normal. Do not heat at this stage.
  2. 02
    Make the sugar syrup. Combine the remaining water and all the sugar in a saucepan. Heat gently over medium-low heat, stirring until fully dissolved. Do not boil. Remove from heat once clear.
  3. 03
    Combine & emulsify. Allow the sugar syrup to cool slightly, then slowly add the hydrated gum arabic mixture while stirring constantly. The gum will incorporate smoothly.
  4. 04
    Strain & bottle. Pass the finished syrup through a fine mesh strainer or cheesecloth to remove any undissolved particles. Bottle in a sterilised glass container.
  5. 05
    Storage. Refrigerate. Lasts 3–4 weeks. The high sugar content acts as a preservative, but gum arabic can ferment over time. Add 1 tsp vodka per 250 ml to extend shelf life.

Notes

  • Use food-grade gum arabic (acacia gum) — available from homebrew or baking suppliers.
  • The syrup should be glossy and slightly thicker than simple syrup when cooled.
  • If the syrup turns cloudy or stringy, the gum didn't fully hydrate — strain again.
  • Works beautifully in Old Fashioneds, Whiskey Sours, and classic Daiquiris.
